---
title: Export
description: Art. 15 collection across your database and external systems — one structured bundle, with failures surfaced, never silently omitted.
---

An Art. 15 answer has to cover everything you hold on a subject — local
tables *and* the external systems that hold PII on your behalf. The
`Exporter` walks the [data map](../manifest/) for local data and fans out
to registered [resolvers](../resolvers/) for the rest:

```python
exporter = Exporter(data_map, graph, Base.metadata, audit, registry)
bundle = exporter.export_subject(session, "42", refs=(stripe_ref,))
```

`export_subject` only reads — it never writes through your session. It is
a blocking call that drives async resolvers on an internal event loop, so
in async web apps dispatch it via a threadpool (FastAPI:
`run_in_threadpool`, or a plain `def` route).

## The bundle

`ExportBundle` is a frozen pydantic model:

- `subject_id` and `generated_at` (UTC).
- `schema_version` — the [manifest schema version](../manifest/) the
  bundle was built under, so downstream tooling can interpret old bundles
  correctly.
- `records` — one `ExportRecord` per exported value, local and external
  alike: `source` (table or resolver name), `field`, `category`, the
  `value` itself, and the Art. 15 metadata declared in the annotations —
  `legal_basis`, `purpose`, `retention_reason`. Recording *why* data is
  held is required Art. 15(1)(a) metadata; effaced surfaces exactly what
  you declared.
- `incomplete_sources` — see below.

Group and render `records` however your product needs;
`bundle.model_dump(mode="json")` gives a JSON-ready payload.

## The `incomplete_sources` contract

A resolver call that fails does **not** fail the export and does **not**
silently shrink the bundle — the resolver's name lands in
`incomplete_sources`. A bundle with a non-empty `incomplete_sources` is an
honest partial answer: you know exactly which system is missing and can
retry or escalate. Silent omission would be the worst outcome — a subject
(or a regulator) reading a bundle as complete when it isn't.

The boundary cases are deliberate:

- A ref whose `kind` matches no registered resolver raises
  `ResolverError` *before* any work or audit event — a typo'd kind must
  never silently drop an external source from the answer.
- A registered resolver with **no** matching ref is *skipped*: "the
  subject has no identity in that system" is a complete answer, recorded
  in the `EXPORT_COMPLETED` audit payload's `skipped_resolvers`, never in
  `incomplete_sources`.
- A local database failure propagates as an exception after
  `EXPORT_REQUESTED` was appended — a requested-but-never-completed trail
  is the abandonment marker.

## Audit trail

Every export appends `EXPORT_REQUESTED` before collection and
`EXPORT_COMPLETED` after, with record counts, incomplete sources, and
skipped resolvers in the payload — counts and names only, never the
exported values. Input validation failures raise before any event: a
malformed call never became a data-subject request.

:::note
What lands in a bundle is exactly what the manifest declares. An
unannotated column is not exported, and changing what gets exported — by
annotation or by library behaviour — is MAJOR under
[widened SemVer](../../project/stability/). Whether your bundle satisfies
Art. 15 for your processing is a determination for you and your counsel.
:::
